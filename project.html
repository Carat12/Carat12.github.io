Walkability Visualization and Clustering

Walkability, in simple terms, indicates how suitable an area is for walking. Walkability has relationships with many factors. Research find that high walkability is related with low crime rate and high health level in neighborhoods. An interactive web page was designed to present the distribution of walkability over Erie County, Buffalo, NY at the level of census block groups. Also walkability clustering is implemented and you can customize the threshold of walkability and number of clusters.


Tools:  ArcGIS Javascript API 4.16, HTML, CSS

Spatial Autocorrelation of Racial Segregation

Racial Segregation refers to the physical separation of different racial groups into different neighborhoods. Spatial Autocorrelation evaluates the degree to which observations at spatial locations are similar to each other. This project measured the racial segregation between three sets of racial groups and analyzed the spatial autocorrelation of racial segregation for each set to reveal where similar levels of segregation cluster and where dissimilar levels of segregation appear.


Tools:  ArcGIS Desktop 10.5

Racial Skewing in Police Traffic Stops

A police traffic stop, also commonly called being pulled over, is a temporary detention of a driver by police to investigate a possible crime or minor violation of law. Would the possibility of being stopped by police be related with driver's race? This project evaluated a weighted ratio of drivers of different racial groups stopped by police in FL, and mapped the results to reveal the existence of racial skewing.


Tools:  R, Markdown
